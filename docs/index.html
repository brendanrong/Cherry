Cherry

A menu bar manager for macOS.

Download Cherry

Requires macOS 14 or later · Free · Open source (GPL-3.0)

Cherry is a fork of Ice by Jordan Baird, rebranded with macOS 26 fixes. The menu-bar engine is all Ice's work. For the original, actively maintained app, use Ice.

What it does

  • Hide, show, and rearrange menu bar items
  • An "always-hidden" section for things you rarely need
  • Reveal hidden items on hover, click, or scroll
  • Drag-and-drop layout, plus a separate bar for notch Macs
  • Search your menu bar items
  • Customize the menu bar's tint, shadow, border, and shape
  • Global hotkeys, launch at login, automatic updates

Install

  • Download and open Cherry.dmg, then drag Cherry into Applications.
  • First launch: macOS will say it "could not verify" Cherry (it's signed but not notarized). Click Done (not Move to Bin!), then go to System Settings > Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway. One-time thing.
  • Then grant Accessibility and Screen Recording in System Settings so it can manage the menu bar.
